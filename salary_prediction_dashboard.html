How the Model Works
1

Training Data

Gradient boosting model trained on 2021–2025 filled requisitions. Outliers removed prior to training. Data segmented by country — each country receives its own model.

2

Prediction Grain

Predictions are made at the intersection of Job Code × Compensation Grade × Location. This is the finest grain at which historical pay patterns are statistically reliable.

3

Feature Engineering

Key model inputs: historical median pay per group, year-over-year pay trend, grade-level pay band, and requisition volume (data density signal). Log-transform applied to base pay to normalize the skewed salary distribution.

4

Validation Approach

Time-based split: trained on 2021–2024, validated on 2025. 2026 YTD held as out-of-sample test. Random cross-validation was intentionally avoided to prevent salary-level leakage across years.

5

New Hire Application

Model tested against May–June open requisitions. HR estimate and model prediction compared to historical average for the same job × grade × location group.

⚠️
Model limitations to disclose: Predictions for job × grade × location combinations with fewer than 5 historical records fall back to a global median estimate and carry higher uncertainty. The model does not account for individual candidate qualifications, market compression events, or off-cycle pay adjustments. This model is a budgeting tool — final offer decisions remain with HR and Finance leadership.
